    About this tool

    About this tool

    This tool provides Massachusetts educational leaders with a comprehensive framework to audit existing district AI policies and refine strategic guidance for the Human-Centered, ethical use of artificial intelligence.

    Design intention
    Design intention
    Select to view →
    The experience is intentionally structured to distinguish what district leaders need to do from what the system generates in response. Input areas are clearly labeled as action spaces. Report areas are visually differentiated as generated policy review outputs.
    Framework integrity
    Framework integrity
    Select to view →
    The tool aggregates six governance pillars across macro, meso, and micro levels. It adds a human-centered lens that checks whether AI is framed as supporting rather than replacing educators, while protecting dignity, trust, equity, and wellbeing.

        Policy language tone analysis

        This layer reads the policy through three rhetorical modes — compliance-oriented, aspirational, and operational — and measures how much of the language falls into each register. Operational language drives the most reliable implementation.

        Compliance mode
        0%
        Language oriented toward legal obligations, prohibitions, and regulatory alignment. Necessary but insufficient on its own for human-centered governance.
        Aspirational mode
        0%
        Language expressing values, commitments, and intentions without specifying how they will be enacted. Important for setting direction but insufficient alone.
        Operational mode
        0%
        Language specifying named roles, defined processes, timelines, and concrete actions. This is the register most associated with consistent district-wide implementation.

        Implementation readiness measures whether a policy can actually be put into practice — whether it names who is responsible, when things happen, how decisions get made, and how families and educators will know what to do.
